💰 Budget Builder

Semi-monthly paycheck planner with tax estimation, 401(k) front-loading & debt payoff

📖 How to Use Budget Builder

Step 1: Enter Your Income

Enter your annual salary, select your filing status and state. The tool calculates your gross pay per paycheck (salary ÷ 24).

If your paycheck withholds extra federal tax (for RSUs, bonuses, etc.), enter that in W-4 Extra Withholding.

Step 2: Set Your IRS Limits

Click ⚙️ IRS Contribution Limits to select the tax year. Limits for 2024–2026 are built in. Future years show a warning and use the latest available.

Step 3: Pre-Tax Deductions

These reduce your taxable income:

  • 401(k) Employee % — your pre-tax contribution as % of gross
  • Employer Match % — percentage of your contribution the employer matches (e.g. 50% means they add $0.50 per $1 you contribute)
  • HSA — your per-paycheck amount + employer annual lump sum
  • AD&D Insurance — per paycheck

Step 4: Post-Tax Deductions

These come out of your paycheck but do NOT reduce taxes:

  • After-Tax 401(k) % — mega backdoor Roth contributions
  • ESPP % — employee stock purchase plan
  • Legal Plan / LTD Offset — per paycheck amounts

Step 5: Review Tax Breakdown

See your estimated federal, Social Security, Medicare, state, and WA Cares taxes per paycheck. These are estimates based on tax brackets — your actual withholding depends on your W-4.

Step 6: 401(k) Front-Load Tracker

If you contribute to a 401(k), the tracker shows:

  • Separate progress bars for employee pre-tax, employer match, and after-tax
  • Combined 415(c) stacked bar ($72k limit for 2026)
  • Exact paycheck # and date when each limit is hit
  • Side-by-side take-home comparison: during front-loading vs after max-out

Step 7: Add Bills, Spending & Savings

Add your monthly expenses. Click + Add to add rows, × to remove. These are used by the cash flow analysis.

Step 8: Annual Cash Flow Analysis

Because front-loading means uneven paychecks, this section shows:

  • Blended annual take-home — what you actually get across both phases
  • Monthly budget allowance — annual ÷ 12, the safe amount to spend per month
  • Savings buffer needed — cash reserve for the lean front-loading months
  • 12-month grid — green/red per month showing surplus or deficit

Step 9: Debt Payoff Planner

Add debts with name, type, balance, APR, minimum payment, and optional target payoff date. The tool:

  • Calculates the required monthly payment to hit your target date
  • Checks if it's feasible within your surplus
  • Compares Avalanche (highest interest first) vs Snowball (smallest balance first) strategies

Step 10: Export to Excel

Click Export Budget to Excel to download a styled spreadsheet. You can upload an existing .xlsx file to append a new sheet — great for tracking budgets month over month.

💡 Tip: All calculations update instantly as you type. Start with your salary and work your way down — the tool builds on each section.
